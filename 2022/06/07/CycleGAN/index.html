CycleGAN

Posted on 2022-06-07

Introduction

John is traveling from the US to Japan. However, he has zero knowledge on Japanese, and he is trying to choose a translator for himself during the trip. How could he measure the performance of a translator that translates from English to Japanese and the other way around, without knowing any Japanese at all?

One of the solutions, is that John can come up with a few English sentences, translate them into Japanese, then translate these intermediate results back to English. He can then compare the original sentences with the results, and the closer they look like, the better the translator performs.

This is the basic idea for CycleGAN. Besides trying to fool the discriminator on the other domain, the model also converts the generated output back to the original domain and check the difference from the original input.
